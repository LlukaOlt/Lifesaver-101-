What should I do if I get a minor cut?

First, wash your hands to prevent infection. Then, gently clean the wound with water and mild soap. Apply an antiseptic and cover it with a sterile bandage.

How do I stop bleeding from a cut?

Apply firm pressure to the cut using a clean cloth or bandage. Keep the area elevated if possible. Most minor cuts stop bleeding within a few minutes.

When should I see a doctor for a cut?

  • If the cut is deep or won't stop bleeding after 10 minutes of applying pressure.
  • If the cut shows signs of infection such as redness, swelling, or pus.
  • If the cut was caused by a dirty object, like a rusty nail, and there is a risk of tetanus.

How can I prevent infection in a cut?

Keep the cut clean and dry, and change the bandage regularly. Avoid touching the wound with dirty hands and apply antiseptic when necessary.

How do I know if my cut is infected?

Signs of infection include increased pain, redness, warmth, swelling, pus, or a fever. If you notice these symptoms, consult a healthcare professional promptly.
