What is an allergic reaction?
Common allergens include pollen, dust mites, pet dander, certain foods, insect stings, and medications.
What is anaphylaxis?
Anaphylaxis is a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction that requires immediate medical attention. Symptoms include throat swelling, difficulty breathing, and a drop in blood pressure.
Can you develop allergies later in life?
Yes, allergies can develop at any age, even if you’ve never had them before.
How can I find out what I’m allergic to?
Allergy testing, such as skin prick tests or blood tests, can help identify specific allergens.
Can allergies be cured?
While there is no cure for allergies, treatments like immunotherapy (allergy shots) can help reduce sensitivity to allergens over time.
